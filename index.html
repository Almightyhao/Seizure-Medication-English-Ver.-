2. What if I forget to take my medicine?

  • ‚û§ Golden Rule: Take it as soon as you remember.
  • ‚û§ Exception: If it is close to your next dose (more than halfway through the interval), skip the missed dose and take the next one as scheduled.
  • ABSOLUTELY NEVER DOUBLE THE DOSE!
  • Note - Fycompa: Due to its very long half-life (~105 hrs), if you miss 1 day, do not make it up. Just take the regular dose the next day. Consult doctor if missed multiple days.

3. Side Effects & Interactions

  • Adaptation: Dizziness, sleepiness, or unsteadiness are common in the first 1-2 weeks. Do not stop meds; it usually improves.
  • Danger Signs: Systemic red rash (measles-like), fever, or mouth ulcers. Stop meds and go to ER immediately (Suspected Allergy).
  • Mood: A few may experience anger, anxiety, or depression. Return to the hospital.
  • Interactions: Inform other doctors (e.g., dentists) that you are taking anti-epileptic drugs.
  • No Alcohol: Alcohol triggers seizures and worsens side effects.

4. Special Populations

  • Pregnancy: Never stop meds yourself! Seizures cause hypoxia which hurts the baby more. Discuss with doctor; supplement high-dose Folic Acid.
  • Elderly: Slower metabolism leads to dizziness. Family must assist when walking/using toilet to prevent falls.
  • Safety: Do not swim alone. Avoid climbing heights.
  • Lifestyle: No alcohol, avoid staying up late, reduce stress.
  • Cooking: Use induction cookers instead of open fire. Shower instead of tub baths.
